FPV Payload Drone (IAM3D 2025)

Fully 3D-printed FPV payload drone optimized for rapid fabrication, durability, and instant field repair.

Objective: Design a fully 3D-printed FPV payload drone optimized for rapid fabrication, durability, and instant field repair while maintaining competitive flight performance.

Design: Implemented DFAM principles including print-in-place joints, generative design, and a PLA/TPU struct-core elastomer frame to absorb impact loads without structural failure. Integrated an electromagnet as the payload device.

Analysis & Validation: Modeled motor thrust loading and validated frame stiffness via static displacement FEA (~10 N per motor, ~40 N total) with the battery support constrained, resulting in ~7.9 mm peak deflection at the arm ends. Bench testing and flight validation confirmed stiffness and durability.

Results: Achieved >2:1 thrust-to-weight ratio, ~16 min measured flight time, and ~4.9 kg measured payload capacity. The airframe survived 13 consecutive 40–60 mph impacts and placed in the top third of 20 teams at IAM3D 2025.

Key metrics: >2:1 T/W · ~16 min endurance · ~4.9 kg payload · 13 high-speed impacts survived

Static displacement FEA · Frame stiffness validation

50 lb Combat Robot

High-energy horizontal spinner weapon system engineered within competition safety limits.

Objective: Design and fabricate a compact, high-energy horizontal spinner weapon system for a 50 lb combat robot, balancing kinetic energy, structural integrity, and competition safety limits.

Design: Modeled a single-piece weapon bar and hub assembly optimized for inertia and manufacturability, integrated with bearing-supported shaft constraints representative of real drivetrain conditions.

Analysis: Performed centrifugal loading analysis using von Mises stress criteria at operating speed, applying local mesh refinement at the hub to capture peak stresses and validate geometry under high rotational loads.

Results: Engineered a horizontal spinner operating at ~25 kJ within NHRL safety parameters, informing material selection, hub geometry, and operating RPM for reliable competition use.

Key metrics: ~25 kJ weapon energy · NHRL-compliant · Hub geometry validated via FEA

Centrifugal loading FEA · Von Mises stress

FPV Rover Drivetrain (IAM3D 2026)

Fully 3D-printed tracked drivetrain designed for abrasive terrain and modular iteration.

Objective: Design a fully 3D-printed tracked drivetrain for an IAM3D competition rover, prioritizing robustness in sandy terrain, modularity for rapid iteration, and stability capable of supporting a future payload platform.

Design: Implemented a custom pin-jointed modular track system with sprocket-driven traction and integrated guide features to maintain alignment under load. Drivetrain designed around printable components and off-the-shelf motors and bearings to enable rapid repair and redesign.

Analysis & Validation: Applied conservative worst-case static load approximations to individual track links and sprocket interfaces; validated articulation, engagement, and drivetrain robustness through extensive bench testing and RC operation.

Results: Achieved smooth, repeatable tracked motion with stable engagement and minimal derailment during testing, with easily swappable components demonstrating feasibility for mounting a material collection device for the final IAM3D project.

Key metrics: Robust tracked motion · Minimal derailment · Modular, field-repairable drivetrain

Static load approximations · Bench validation
